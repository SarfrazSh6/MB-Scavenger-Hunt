A6 – Humanities Building

The self-concept is basically your brain’s “About Me” page — the mental storage that holds everything you know about yourself: traits, values, roles, abilities, appearance, and even the sense that you exist as a unique individual. These pieces form self-schemas, the mental categories you use to make sense of anything related to you.

The self-concept includes physical qualities, personality traits, and social identities. Some people have high self-complexity (seeing themselves through many independent roles), while others have high self-concept clarity (a clear and stable sense of self).
