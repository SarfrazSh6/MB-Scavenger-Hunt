A2 – Statistical / Quantified Self

In A2, the statistical self comes into focus. Here, students turn biological variation and personal behavior into numbers: microbial abundance, diversity indices, p-values, or survey scores. When you quantify patterns, you start seeing identity through graphs, distributions, and effect sizes.

Readings on microbiome data analysis and the “second genome” highlight how much of the inner self can be expressed in tables and plots. The quantified self enables prediction, monitoring, and evidence-based decisions – but it also simplifies experience. Numbers can show trends without capturing emotions, motivations, or lived context.

A2 symbolizes this shift from intuitive, subjective understanding to analytical reasoning. It is where the self is translated into variables, where evidence is weighed, and where you learn both the power and the limits of seeing your life as data.
