Palms / John Sexton Square – Environmental Self

The environmental self is the idea that identity stretches into the physical and social spaces we inhabit. John Sexton Square, with palms, sunlight, and constant pedestrian flow, is where students experience themselves in relation to open air and public space.

Readings on human–microbe interactions and life history plasticity show that humans are deeply responsive to environmental cues. Light, greenery, social presence, and movement patterns all shape stress regulation and emotional state. Sitting in shaded natural areas can lower cortisol and increase feelings of clarity and grounding.

Microbiome and social readings emphasize that outdoor environments and microbes are always interacting with our bodies. Exposure to diverse environmental microbes can support immune and microbial diversity, while public plazas also influence how we present ourselves and negotiate social identity. John Sexton Square becomes a node where physical, microbial, and social environments co-create who you are in that moment.
