C1 – Experimental Research Building

Germ-free mice — raised without any microbes — were used to solve a drug safety mystery involving brivudine. Normal mice produced high levels of a toxic metabolite known as bromovinyluracil. Germ-free mice produced almost none. This proved that about 70% of the drug’s toxicity was caused by gut microbes, specifically strains like Bacteroides thetaiotaomicron and Bacteroides ovatus, which transformed the safe drug into a harmful compound.
