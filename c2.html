C2 – Health Center: Immune Self

The immune self describes the body’s ability to distinguish self from non-self. C2, the Health Center, mirrors this process: it is where illness, imbalance, and vulnerability are recognized and treated. Immunology readings show that the immune system constantly evaluates signals, tolerating beneficial microbes while responding to threats.

Modern immunology views immunity as a dynamic ecosystem rather than a rigid wall. It communicates with the nervous system and microbiome, and is shaped by stress, sleep, diet, and social exposure. Dysbiosis—disruption of microbial communities—can alter immune signaling and inflammation.

The Health Center supports preventative care, treatment, and guidance that strengthen this system of boundaries and tolerance. C2 therefore represents biological decision-making about what belongs, what is dangerous, and what should be remembered and defended against – all core parts of the immune self.
