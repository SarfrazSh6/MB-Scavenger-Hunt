D2 – Dining Hall: Dietary / Gut Self

Inside D2, the dietary self is constantly being formed and reshaped. Every plate is not just nutrition but identity: culture, stress, habit, and your evolving gut microbiome all show up at the buffet. Course readings on coevolution between humans and microbes show that diet is one of the strongest forces shaping gut communities.

Microbes in the gut signal to the brain, influencing mood, appetite, energy, and even decision making. Patterns like plant-based eating, heavy refined carbs, skipping meals, or following cultural foods all push the microbiome toward balance or dysbiosis. Dysbiosis can affect digestion, inflammation, immunity, and emotional well-being.

Food also works symbolically: students often choose meals that reflect upbringing, community, health values, or sustainability commitments. D2 becomes a space where the visceral biological self, the cultural self, and the microbial self all interact over a tray of food.
